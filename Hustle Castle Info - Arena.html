Bracket system


In the Arena your opponents get chosen by your fighters level. Therefore everyone gets divided in so called brackets. Those brackets go from lvl 1 to the bracket level and your strongest fighter decides in wich bracket you wil land in. e.g. if your highest fighter is lvl 53 your bracketlevel is 55

Bracket levels you can land in


Following bracket levels exist:

20, 30, 40, 47, 55, 62, 70, 77, 87, 100

You can have Fighters exceeding the bracketlevel if you follow the rules below:

Rules to have Fighters above bracket level


1. The average of your [barracks level] fighters have to be below the bracket level +1

2. fighters cant be over the next bracketlevel e.g. for 5x55 cant be over 62

3. If a fighter is over your bracketlevel no other fighter can be equal or below [Rounded down((bracket level-5)/5)*5] e.g. for 5x55 if a fighter is >55 no other fighter can be equal or below 50 // for 5x62 if a fighter is >62 no other fighter can be equal or below 55

4. for barracks level 3 are 0, 4 are 1, 5 are 2 and 6 are 3 fighters alowed to be above bracket level

Bracket system Tool


Just type in the level of your strongest fighters and your barracks level to see in wich bracket your team will go.

The "Chip and Dale" and the "Tom and Jerry" offer breaks those rules!

Abbreviation


On forums you will often see something like 5x55 In combination with the arena its an abbreviation and it stands for the brackat someone can be in. The first number is the barracks level and the second number is the Brackat level.

Tactics


First I have to say you wont win often if you just follow a simple guide, you have to try and learn. After a while you will find more and more tricks that help you all of wich are to specific to mention here. But they can help you a t least at the beginning, so here are the 3 most used Tactics:

Waiting game


Round 1: Dont attack.
Round 2: Attacke someone who is in front of you and who wont be in front of you in round 3.
Round 3: Attacke someone who is in front of you and who wont be in front of you in round 4.
Round 4: Attack so you have a weak opponent in front of you in the last round.
Round 5: Attack the weak opponent in front of you.

Pros.:

- Easy to use
- Low probability an allready fought opponent gets in front of you

Cons.:

- Sometimes you cant catch up to the ones in front of you

The fool


Round 1: Attacke the 4th weakest.
Round 2: If no one is in front of you attack the 3rd weakest otherwise attack to have someone weaker in front of you for round 3.
Round 3: If no one is in front of you attack the 2nd weakest otherwise attack to have someone weaker in front of you for round 2.
Round 4: If no one is in front of you lose points to have someone weaker in front the next round otherwise attack the weakest.
Round 5: Attack the weak opponent in front of you.

Pros.:

- Often you get more points in the first 3 rounds
- You have more control if you are at teh top

Cons.:

- Only with relatively strong team viable

The king


Round 1: Attack the second strongest.
Round 2: Attack the weakest in front of you.
Round 3: If possible attack someone in front of you or the weakest.
Round 4: If possible attack someone in front of you or the weakest.
Round 5: If possible attack someone in front of you or the weakest.

Pros.:

- Weaker opponents are often at the top in the beginning that leads to more points if you attack early
- You have more control if you are at teh top

Cons.:

- Only with strongest team viable
